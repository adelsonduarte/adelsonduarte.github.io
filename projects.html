Projects that I have been involved with

An IoT energy consumption system based on Powerline communication.

his project was a result of my masters. It can be split into circuit design/firmware development and web service application. The circuit shown below uses a microcontroller MSP430AFE253 from Texas Instruments (TI), a switching power supply based on UCC28910 (TI), analog circuits to measure voltage and current, to modulate and demodulate measured data and set it properly within powerlines.

Firmware programming was made to acquire voltage and current samples, output PWM carrier signal and digitalized data (UART) for modulation circuit, to control the amplifier gain of the demodulation stage and select the carrier frequency by sensing the channel noise.

In order to verify the waveform of the measured signal, I did a test bench using LABVIEW, as shown below.

Finally, the modulation and demodulation circuit was duplicated and attached on Raspberry Pi. Both module were connected to the powerlines, and the measured digitalized data was received by Raspberry Pi and sent to a Flask web service, based on REST, using python language.

Circuit design for autonomous sensor in industry 4.0

This work was done in a public-private cooperation between a local company and FAPESP. The main objective was evaluate the application of thermal Energy Harvesting to supply an autonomous wireless sensor node. I was responsible for designing a power management circuit that interfaces the thermoelectric generator and the wireless sensor node. Three DC-DC converter were evaluated: LTC3108 (Analog Devices), MP3414 (Monolithic Power Systems) and BQ25570 (TI). The circuit shown below is a DC-DC converter using LTC3108 that presented the best performance in our evaluation. A supercapacitor was used to store the harvested energy and to supply the autonomous sensor.

Winner of the 2nd CubeDesign! Cubsat project

I participated of the 2nd CubeDesign Cubsat project organizated by the National Institute for Space Research (INPE) by means of participating in an extracurricular team at Unicamp in space-modeling. In this project, I was responsible for the Energy harvesting module. The energy harvested by solar panels was used to recharge the Cubesat's battery pack. The current and the voltage of the bateries were processed by STM32F103. The developed Cubesat was subjected to a series of tests, including sensor telemetry, control of a reaction wheel and antenna ejection. The team that demonstrated the best outcome was ours and as a result, we received the first place
